Understanding Corporate Bankruptcy

Bankruptcy is a critical financial state where a corporation is unable to honor its debt obligations. When a company's liabilities exceed the fair market value of its assets, it often leads to insolvency, causing massive losses for investors, lenders, and employees. Predicting this state is vital for maintaining global financial stability.

⚠️ Why Prediction Matters?

Early detection of financial distress through ratios like ROA, Debt-to-Equity, and Working Capital allows stakeholders to take preventive measures, saving billions in potential liquidation losses.
